# ✅ Daily Check-In — Patient App Feature

## What It Is

The Daily Check-In is the mechanism by which Swasthya AI maintains continuous patient monitoring between doctor visits. Every day, the system generates 2–3 personalised questions for the patient to answer. These questions are not generic — they are derived from the patient's specific conditions, recent symptom history, active medications, and the last 24 hours of wearable data.

The check-in is the primary data collection engine of the platform. It is the reason the system knows, over time, whether a patient is genuinely improving or whether a slow deterioration is building under the surface.

---

## Question Generation

Questions are generated by the AI backend at the start of each day (or on-demand when triggered by an event). The generation prompt includes:

- Patient's chronic conditions and severity
- Symptoms reported in the past 7 days
- Current medications and any missed doses
- Wearable anomalies detected in the past 24 hours
- Doctor's pending questions from the async Q&A loop
- Any risk score changes from the previous day

### Examples of Generated Questions

**For a hypertensive patient whose smartwatch showed elevated resting HR overnight:**
*"Did you experience any chest tightness or breathlessness last night or this morning?"*

**For a diabetic patient who missed their insulin dose yesterday:**
*"How are you feeling today? Did anything prevent you from taking your insulin dose yesterday?"*

**For a patient who reported fatigue for three consecutive days:**
*"Your fatigue has continued for a few days. Has it been affecting your ability to do everyday activities?"*

**Generic (when no specific triggers):**
*"How would you rate your overall energy today, on a scale of 1 to 10?"*

---

## Question Types

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Severity scale | 1–10 numeric self-rating | "Rate your joint pain today" |
| Yes/No with follow-up | Binary with optional detail | "Did you sleep well? If no, why not?" |
| Free text | Open-ended for nuanced response | "Describe how you've been feeling since yesterday" |
| Symptom check | Specific symptom presence | "Are you still experiencing the nausea you mentioned on Monday?" |
| Adherence check | Medicine-specific | "Did you take your metformin this morning?" |

---

## Event-Triggered Check-Ins

In addition to the daily morning check-in, the system generates **event-triggered check-ins** when:

| Trigger | Check-In Content |
|---|---|
| Wearable anomaly detected (SQI-validated) | Question about symptoms related to the anomaly |
| Risk score increases significantly (≥ 10 points) | Targeted symptom questions for that risk factor |
| Medication missed (consecutive 2+) | Adherence and side-effect check |
| Doctor queued a question via async Q&A loop | Doctor's question rephrased conversationally |
| Recovery Confirmation triggered | Wellbeing confirmation and lifestyle questions |

Event-triggered check-ins appear as in-app notifications: *"Quick check — we noticed something. Can you answer one question?"*

---

## Answering the Check-In

The patient sees a clean card-based UI — one question at a time, large text, large tap targets. The flow is:

1. Question shown with appropriate input (slider, Yes/No buttons, text field)
2. Patient answers and taps Next
3. Optional follow-up if the answer triggers one (e.g. "Yes, chest tightness" → "Is it happening right now or was it earlier?")
4. Summary card shown at the end: *"Thanks. We've noted your answers. Your check-in is complete for today."*

The entire check-in typically takes under 60 seconds.

---

## What Happens With Answers

After submission, each answer is processed:

```
Check-in submitted
        │
        ▼
Structured data extraction per answer
        │
        ▼
Update relevant symptom records
        │
        ▼
Recalculate risk score
        │
        ▼
Update body heatmap zone scores
        │
        ▼
Check escalation matrix
        │
        ▼
If doctor had a pending question → mark answered → notify doctor
        │
        ▼
Update morning briefing for doctor
```

## Missed Check-In Handling

If a patient misses 2 or more consecutive check-ins:
- Flagged in the doctor's morning briefing under "Low Engagement"
- Event-triggered check-in sent: *"We haven't heard from you in a couple of days. How are you doing?"*
- Risk score receives a small penalty for missing data (the system cannot assume silence means fine)

Missing check-ins are never silently ignored. The system is designed to treat non-response as a signal that needs to be followed up on — not as clean data.

---

## Doctor Pending Questions Integration

When a doctor submits a question via the async Q&A loop and the answer is not found in existing data, the system:

1. Rewrites the doctor's clinical question in natural, conversational language
2. Adds it to the patient's next check-in as an additional question
3. When the patient answers, the answer is:
   - Stored in `doctor_questions` with the response
   - Linked back to the doctor's original question
   - The doctor is notified: *"[Patient name]'s response to your question is ready."*

This closes the loop between the doctor's curiosity and the patient's lived experience — without the patient needing to know a doctor specifically asked.
